FC Barcelona

FC Barcelona, also known as Barça, is one of the most famous and influential football clubs in the world. Based in Barcelona, Spain, the club has a long rich history with scintillating achievements on and off the pitch. FC Barcelona nicknamed "Blaugrana".

FC Barcelona was founded on 29 November 1899 by a group of English, Swiss and Spanish footballers. The club is known for its attractive style of play and strong identity. The club's distinctive colours are blue and red, and its famous stadium, Camp Nou, has become one of the world's sporting icons. They are also known as a team that attaches importance to the development of young players through their famous football academy, La Masia.

La Liga

La Liga broadly speaking, is the highest professional league in the football league competition system in Spain. The league is held annually, participated by 20 professional clubs, where the three lowest-ranked clubs at the end of the season will be relegated to Segunda División and their places will be replaced by the two top-ranked clubs and play-off winners in Segunda División.

Copa Del Rey

The Campeonato de España Copa de Su Majestad el Rey, commonly known as the Spanish King's Cup or Copa del Rey, is an annual knockout men's football competition in Spain organized by the Royal Spanish Football Federation. It was formed in 1903 and is the oldest football competition in Spain at the national level.

Super Copa De Espana

The Supercopa de España is a Spanish football championship that brings together the champions of La Liga and the Copa del Rey. The competition did not start until 1982, but between 1940 and 1953, several other tournaments were played between the Spanish league champions and cup winners.

UEFA Super Cup

The UEFA Super Cup from 1972 to 1994 named the European Super Cup was a match between European football clubs contested by teams that were the winners of the UEFA Champions League and the winners of the UEFA Europa League. Prior to the 1999/2000 season, the UEFA Super Cup always pitted the winners of the Champions League against the winners of the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup.

Champions League

The UEFA Champions League (LCE), formerly known as the European Cup, is a club football competition contested by UEFA and contested by clubs from Europe's highest league or division. It is one of the most prestigious tournaments in the world and the most prestigious club competition in European football, contested only by national league champions (as well as runners-up for some countries) from each UEFA member national association.

FIFA Club World Cup

The FIFA Club World Cup, formerly known as the FIFA Club World Championship, is an official competition held by FIFA that brings together the winners of major championships between football clubs from every continent. It was not held from 2001 to 2004 due to a combination of factors in the cancellation of the 2001 tournament, most importantly the collapse of marketing partner FIFA International Sport and Leisure (ISL), but since 2005 it is held annually, and has been hosted by Brazil, Japan, United Arab Emirates , Morocco, and Qatar
